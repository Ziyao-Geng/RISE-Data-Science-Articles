# RISE Data Science Article Roadmap

## Meeting Direction

Based on the 12 May 2026 RISE meeting notes, the data science education project should:

- Start with website articles before live workshops.
- Use a fictional sample dataset so learners can follow a full analysis workflow safely.
- Teach practical beginner skills in R, RStudio, Quarto, data cleaning, and analysis.
- Publish 2-3 strong articles first, then record short videos.
- Use the articles and videos as preparation for later in-person workshops.

## Article Sequence

### Article 1: Getting Started with R for Medical Research

Status: drafted and organized.

Folder: `articles/01_getting_started_with_r/`

Purpose: help complete beginners install the tools, understand R/RStudio/Quarto, load a simple health dataset, inspect it, calculate summaries, and make first plots.

### Article 2: Cleaning Clinical Research Data in R

Status: drafted and organized.

Folder: `articles/02_data_cleaning/`

Purpose: teach a practical workflow for protecting raw data, checking missing values, cleaning names, standardising categories, identifying duplicates, handling suspicious values, saving cleaned data, and documenting decisions.

### Article 3: Introductory Analysis and Reporting in R

Status: proposed.

Suggested folder: `articles/03_intro_analysis_reporting/`

Purpose: show learners how to move from cleaned data to simple research outputs: descriptive tables, grouped summaries, basic plots, simple statistical tests, and a short Quarto report.

Potential sections:

- Load the cleaned teaching dataset.
- Define a simple research question.
- Create a table of participant characteristics.
- Compare groups using beginner-friendly tests.
- Create clear figures for a website or report.
- Write results in plain clinical research language.
- Export tables, figures, and a reproducible Quarto report.

## Later Content Ideas

- Using AI tools carefully for code formatting, explanation, and debugging.
- How to make a reproducible research folder.
- How to choose common statistical tests.
- Turning an article into a short recorded tutorial.
- Preparing hands-on activities for future RISE workshops.

## Next Steps

1. Review Articles 1 and 2 for tone, accuracy, and consistency.
2. Send 2-3 article ideas to the RISE team for approval.
3. Draft Article 3 after the first two are approved.
4. After three articles are ready, plan short companion videos.
